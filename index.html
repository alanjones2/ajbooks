I can't tell you how to be a better writer but I know someone who can

This is an article soon to be published on Medium where I look at three authors and the books that they have written about how to write well.
I look at three literary experts and their books: the best-selling novalist Stephen King, the Pychologist, Steven Pinker, and the legendary journalist and editor, Harold Evans.

  • On Writing, by Stephen King, is a very personal book about King's life, how he developed as a writer and how he applies his craft. It is also, as you migh expect from a master story-teller, a very entertaining book.
  • A Sense of Style, by Steven Pinker, is an analytical look and the way others write and what lessons we can learn from them. Pinker also stresses the importance of grammar and maintains that a book has to be designed in order to possess an arc of coherence.
  • Do I Make Myself Clear?, by Harold Evans, is the result of Evans's many years as an editor. He describes the tools of the trade and then tells us how to apply them. His aim is clarity and conciseness.

Plotting with Pandas: An Introduction to Data Visualization in Python

A book to download

If you are a Data Scientist or Data Journalist, being able to visualize your data gives you the ability to better understand and communicate it.
This book introduces the plot functions that are part of Python Pandas.
You can download it from this repo. It comes as a PDF for printing or displaying on a large screen or an EPUB for your tablet, phone or ereader. All of the code in the book and the data files that are used are also available here.
